Pozole Rojo

Cuisine: Mexican Total: 2 hours Servings: 8 Difficulty: Medium Rating: 4.8 / 5

Pozole Rojo is a deeply traditional Mexican stew built around tender pork shoulder and plump, chewy hominy in a vibrant red chile broth. Served at celebrations, holidays, and family gatherings across Mexico for centuries, this pre-Columbian dish is as much about the communal table of garnishes as it is about the soup itself. Each bowl is personalized with shredded cabbage, radishes, oregano, and a generous squeeze of lime.

Ingredients

  • 1kg bone-in pork shoulder, cut into 5cm chunks
  • 1 medium white onion, halved
  • 6 cloves garlic (4 whole, 2 minced)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tbsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 litres water or pork stock
  • 6 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried chiles de arbol (for extra heat, optional)
  • 1 tsp dried Mexican oregano
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 cans (800g total) hominy, drained and rinsed
  • Salt to taste
  • For garnish:
  • Shredded green cabbage
  • Thinly sliced radishes
  • Diced white onion
  • Dried Mexican oregano
  • Lime wedges
  • Tostadas or tortilla chips
  • Sliced avocado

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook the pork: Place pork shoulder, halved onion, 4 whole garlic cloves, bay leaves, and peppercorns in a large stockpot. Cover with 2 litres of water. Bring to a boil, skimming the foam that rises to the surface. Reduce heat and simmer, partially covered, for 1.5 hours until the pork is fork-tender and falling off the bone.
  2. Toast and soak the chiles: While the pork simmers, heat a dry comal or heavy skillet over medium heat. Press each chile flat against the surface for 15-20 seconds per side until fragrant and slightly darkened. Place toasted chiles in a bowl, cover with boiling water, and soak for 20 minutes until softened.
  3. Blend the chile sauce: Drain the chiles, reserving 120ml of soaking liquid. Transfer to a blender with the reserved liquid, 2 minced garlic cloves, oregano, and cumin. Blend until completely smooth, about 2 minutes. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve to remove any remaining skin pieces.
  4. Prepare the pork: Remove pork from the broth. Discard bones, onion, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Shred the meat into bite-sized pieces using two forks. Strain the broth and return it to the pot.
  5. Build the pozole: Add the chile sauce to the strained broth and stir to combine. Add the shredded pork and drained hominy. Bring to a simmer and cook for 20 minutes to meld all the flavors together. Season generously with salt.
  6. Serve with garnishes: Ladle the pozole into deep bowls. Set out all the garnishes on a large platter so each person can customize their bowl. Squeeze lime over the top, add a handful of shredded cabbage, sliced radishes, diced onion, a pinch of oregano, and serve with tostadas on the side.

Chef Tips

  • Chile selection: Guajillo chiles provide the vibrant red color and mild fruity flavor, while ancho chiles add depth and sweetness. Together they create the signature pozole rojo taste without overwhelming heat.
  • Skim the broth: Diligently skim the foam during the first 10 minutes of simmering the pork. This results in a cleaner, clearer broth that lets the chile flavor shine through.
  • Dried hominy: If you can find dried hominy (maiz pozolero), soak it overnight and cook it with the pork from the start. It has a far superior texture and corn flavor compared to canned.
  • Better the next day: Pozole improves significantly after resting overnight in the refrigerator. The flavors deepen and meld. Reheat gently and serve with fresh garnishes.
  • Garnishes are not optional: The garnish table is integral to pozole. The crisp raw cabbage, sharp radishes, and acidic lime juice provide essential contrast to the rich, slow-cooked broth.
