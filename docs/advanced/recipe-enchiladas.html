Chicken Enchiladas

Cuisine: Mexican Prep: 20 min Cook: 25 min Total: 45 min Servings: 6 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.7 / 5 (2,213 reviews)

Enchiladas are the heart of Mexican home cooking — corn tortillas rolled around savory fillings, blanketed in rich chile sauce, and baked until bubbling. The word "enchilar" means "to season with chile," and the star of this dish is the sauce: a complex, earthy blend of dried guajillo and ancho chiles that bears no resemblance to the canned versions found on supermarket shelves. Filled with tender shredded chicken and topped with melted cheese, sour cream, and fresh cilantro, these enchiladas are a weeknight staple that tastes like a weekend project.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • Enchilada sauce:
  • 4 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 cups chicken broth, warm
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Filling:
  • 600g boneless, skinless chicken thighs (or 3 cups shredded rotisserie chicken)
  • 200g shredded Monterey Jack or Oaxaca cheese
  • 100g shredded sharp cheddar cheese
  • 1 can (400g) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 12 corn tortillas (15cm)
  • Toppings:
  • Sour cream or Mexican crema
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Diced white onion
  • Lime wedges
  • Pickled jalapenos (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Toast and rehydrate the chiles: Heat a dry skillet over medium heat. Press each chile flat against the pan for 15-20 seconds per side until fragrant and slightly darkened (do not burn — burnt chiles turn bitter). Transfer to a bowl, cover with the warm chicken broth, and soak for 20 minutes until soft and pliable.
  2. Cook the chicken: While chiles soak, place chicken thighs in a saucepan, cover with salted water, and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook 18-20 minutes until the internal temperature reaches 74C (165F). Remove, let cool slightly, then shred into bite-sized pieces using two forks. Season with a pinch of salt and cumin.
  3. Blend the sauce: Transfer the rehydrated chiles and their soaking liquid to a blender. Add garlic, onion, tomato paste, cumin, oregano, and vinegar. Blend on high for 2 minutes until completely smooth. Heat oil in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve into the pan (this removes any skin fragments for a silky texture). Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened. Season with salt.
  4. Prepare the tortillas: Preheat oven to 190C (375F). Warm corn tortillas by wrapping in a damp paper towel and microwaving for 30-45 seconds, or heat individually in a dry skillet for 15 seconds per side. Warm tortillas are pliable; cold ones will crack when rolled.
  5. Assemble the enchiladas: Spread 1/2 cup of sauce on the bottom of a 23x33cm (9x13 inch) baking dish. Mix shredded chicken with black beans, half the Monterey Jack cheese, and chopped cilantro. Place about 1/3 cup of filling down the center of each tortilla, roll tightly, and place seam-side down in the dish. Pack them snugly side by side.
  6. Sauce and cheese: Pour the remaining enchilada sauce evenly over the rolled tortillas, making sure to coat the edges (exposed tortilla dries out and becomes tough). Top with remaining Monterey Jack and cheddar cheese.
  7. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 15 minutes. Remove foil and bake an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is melted, bubbly, and lightly golden at the edges. Let rest 5 minutes before serving.
  8. Serve: Top with dollops of sour cream or crema, sliced avocado, fresh cilantro, diced white onion, and a squeeze of lime. Serve with pickled jalapenos on the side for those who want extra heat.

Chef Tips

  • Homemade sauce is non-negotiable: Canned enchilada sauce is a pale imitation of the real thing. The 20 minutes it takes to rehydrate and blend dried chiles is the single biggest upgrade you can make. Guajillo chiles provide fruity, bright heat while ancho adds sweetness and depth.
  • Use chicken thighs, not breasts: Thighs stay moist and flavorful even after shredding and baking. Breast meat dries out quickly in the oven, resulting in a chalky filling. The slight extra fat in thighs is exactly what this dish needs.
  • Warm your tortillas: Cold corn tortillas crack and split when rolled. Warming them makes them pliable and prevents tearing. If you skip this step, you will end up with enchilada rubble instead of neat rolls.
  • Coat every surface with sauce: Exposed tortilla edges become dry and crispy in the oven — and not in a good way. Make sure every bit of tortilla is covered with sauce or cheese. The sauce is both flavor and protection.
  • Make ahead and freeze: Assembled enchiladas (before baking) freeze beautifully for up to 3 months. Cover tightly with foil and freeze. Bake from frozen at 190C for 45 minutes covered, then 15 minutes uncovered.

Drink Pairing

Serve alongside a classic margarita (blanco tequila, fresh lime, triple sec) or a light Mexican lager like Modelo Especial with a salted rim. Agua fresca de jamaica (hibiscus) is the perfect non-alcoholic option.
