Matcha Mochi

Cuisine: Japanese Total: 25 min Yield: 12 pieces Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.5 / 5

Soft, pillowy mochi infused with earthy matcha green tea and filled with sweet red bean paste (anko). This traditional Japanese confection, known as daifuku, has been enjoyed for centuries during tea ceremonies and as an everyday treat. The microwave method makes it surprisingly simple to prepare at home without any special equipment.

Ingredients

  • 200g mochiko (sweet glutinous rice flour, Koda Farms recommended)
  • 2 tbsp high-quality matcha powder (culinary grade)
  • 60g granulated sugar
  • 240ml water
  • Cornstarch or potato starch, for dusting
  • 200g anko (sweet red bean paste), divided into 12 balls of about 15g each
  • Pinch of salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the filling: Scoop red bean paste into 12 small balls (about 15g each). Place on a parchment-lined tray and refrigerate for at least 15 minutes until firm. Pre-shaping the filling makes assembly much easier.
  2. Mix the dough: In a large microwave-safe bowl, whisk together mochiko, matcha powder, sugar, and salt until evenly combined with no lumps. Gradually add water while stirring until a smooth, thin batter forms. Cover loosely with plastic wrap.
  3. Cook in microwave: Microwave on high for 2 minutes. Remove, stir vigorously with a wet spatula. Microwave again for 1 minute. Stir again. Microwave a final 30 seconds. The dough should be translucent, sticky, and very thick.
  4. Cool and portion: Generously dust a work surface with cornstarch. Turn out the hot mochi dough onto the starch. Dust the top with more starch. Once cool enough to handle (about 2 minutes), divide into 12 equal pieces using a bench scraper or scissors dusted with starch.
  5. Fill the mochi: Flatten each piece into a circle about 7cm across. Place a chilled anko ball in the center. Gather the edges upward and pinch to seal, rotating the mochi as you go. Place seam-side down on a parchment-lined tray.
  6. Serve: Dust off excess cornstarch with a pastry brush. Serve at room temperature within a few hours for the best texture. Mochi firms up significantly when refrigerated but can be briefly microwaved to soften again.

Chef Tips

  • Matcha quality matters: Use culinary-grade matcha for baking, not ceremonial grade (which is wasted when cooked). Sift the matcha before mixing to avoid green clumps in the dough.
  • Keep everything starchy: Mochi is extremely sticky. Dust your hands, work surface, and tools generously with cornstarch or potato starch. Work quickly before the dough cools and becomes stiff.
  • Stovetop alternative: If you prefer, cook the batter in a nonstick pan over medium-low heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon for about 8-10 minutes until the dough pulls away from the sides cleanly.
  • Eat same day: Mochi is best eaten within a few hours of making. If you must store them, keep at room temperature in an airtight container for up to 1 day. Refrigeration hardens the texture.
  • Flavor variations: Swap matcha for black sesame powder, strawberry powder, or cocoa powder. Fill with fresh strawberry and anko for ichigo daifuku, or with sweetened peanut butter for a modern twist.
