Classic Margherita Pizza

Cuisine: Italian Prep: 20 min Cook: 12 min Rest: 1 hour (dough) Total: 45 min active Servings: 4 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.9 / 5 (2,341 reviews)

The quintessential Neapolitan pizza — a UNESCO-recognized culinary tradition dating back to 1889, when pizzaiolo Raffaele Esposito created it for Queen Margherita of Italy. The three toppings represent the Italian flag: red tomato, white mozzarella, and green basil.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • 500g tipo 00 flour (Caputo brand recommended)
  • 325ml warm water (65% hydration)
  • 10g fine sea salt
  • 3g active dry yeast
  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 400g San Marzano tomatoes (DOP certified, hand-crushed)
  • 250g mozzarella di bufala (fresh, drained)
  • fresh basil leaves (about 14)
  • Flaky sea salt and freshly cracked black pepper to finish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough: Dissolve yeast in warm water (38C) and let stand 5 minutes until foamy. Combine flour and salt in a large bowl, create a well, and pour in yeast water. Mix until a shaggy dough forms, then knead on a lightly floured surface for 10 minutes until smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky.
  2. First rise: Shape into a ball, place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise at room temperature for 1 hour (or cold-ferment in the fridge for 24-72 hours for deeper flavor).
  3. Divide and shape: Punch down the dough, divide into 4 equal pieces (about 210g each). Shape each into a tight ball, cover, and rest 15 minutes.
  4. Preheat: Place a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet on the highest oven rack. Preheat to the maximum temperature (usually 250-275C / 500-530F) for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Stretch the dough: On a floured surface, press each ball from center outward, leaving a 1cm rim for the cornicione (crust). Gently stretch by draping over your knuckles, rotating until 25-30cm diameter. Do not use a rolling pin.
  6. Top: Transfer to a floured peel. Spread 3 tablespoons of crushed San Marzano tomatoes, leaving the rim bare. Tear mozzarella into pieces and distribute evenly. Drizzle with olive oil.
  7. Bake: Slide onto the preheated stone. Bake 8-12 minutes until the crust is puffed, charred in spots, and the cheese is bubbling with golden brown patches.
  8. Finish: Remove from oven, scatter fresh basil leaves, add a final drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of flaky sea salt. Slice and serve immediately.

Chef Tips

  • Cold fermentation: For the best flavor, cold-ferment the dough in the refrigerator for 24-72 hours. This develops complex, slightly tangy flavors that quick-rise dough cannot achieve.
  • San Marzano tomatoes: Use DOP-certified San Marzano tomatoes — they have lower acidity and sweeter, more concentrated flavor than regular canned tomatoes. Crush by hand for rustic texture.
  • Mozzarella moisture: Tear the mozzarella and place on paper towels for 10 minutes before topping. Excess moisture causes a soggy center.
  • Oven temperature: The hotter the better. A pizza steel retains more heat than a stone and produces better char. If you have a broiler, switch to broil for the last 60 seconds.
  • Don't overload: Less is more. The beauty of Margherita is in restraint — let each ingredient shine.
