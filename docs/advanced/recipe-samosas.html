Vegetable Samosas

Cuisine: Indian Total: 60 min Difficulty: Medium Yield: 12 samosas Rating: 4.7 / 5

Samosas are the undisputed king of Indian street snacks. These triangular pastries feature a shatteringly crispy exterior encasing a warmly spiced filling of potatoes, green peas, and aromatic spices. Originating from the Middle East and perfected across South Asia over centuries, samosas are equally at home as an afternoon chai-time snack or as a showstopping appetizer at any gathering.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cold water, or as needed
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled, peeled, and roughly mashed
  • 1/2 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 tbsp oil for filling
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp amchoor (dry mango powder)
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the dough: Combine flour and salt in a bowl. Rub in ghee with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs. Gradually add cold water, mixing until a stiff but pliable dough forms. Knead for 2-3 minutes until smooth, cover with a damp cloth, and rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add grated ginger and green chilies, sauteing for 30 seconds. Stir in the coriander powder, garam masala, red chili powder, and amchoor. Cook for 1 minute.
  3. Combine the filling: Add the mashed potatoes and green peas to the spice mixture. Mix well, ensuring the spices coat the potatoes evenly. Season with salt, fold in the cilantro, and let the filling cool completely before assembling.
  4. Shape the samosas: Divide the dough into 6 equal balls. Roll each ball into a thin oval about 7 inches long. Cut each oval in half to create two semicircles. Moisten the straight edge with water, fold into a cone shape, and pinch the seam tightly to seal.
  5. Fill and seal: Hold each cone upright and fill with 2 tablespoons of the cooled potato filling. Do not overfill. Moisten the top edges with water and pinch firmly to seal, pressing with a fork for a decorative edge.
  6. Fry the samosas: Heat oil to 160C (325F) in a deep pan or kadhai. The oil should not be too hot as samosas need to cook slowly to achieve flaky layers. Gently slide in 3-4 samosas at a time. Fry for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until deep golden brown and crispy on all sides.
  7. Serve: Drain on paper towels for 2 minutes. Serve hot with tamarind chutney, green mint-cilantro chutney, and a sprinkle of chaat masala.

Chef Tips

  • Dough consistency: The dough must be stiff, not soft like roti dough. Too much water makes the samosa shell chewy instead of flaky. The ghee rubbed into the flour is what creates those crispy layers.
  • Cool the filling: Always let the potato filling cool completely before stuffing. Warm filling will soften the dough and cause the samosas to crack during frying.
  • Low and slow frying: Fry at 160C (325F), not higher. High heat will brown the outside before the pastry cooks through, leaving a doughy interior. Patience yields the perfect shatter.
  • Sealing is critical: Ensure every seam is pinched tight with no gaps. Any opening will allow oil to seep in, making the samosa greasy and causing it to burst open during frying.
  • Baked alternative: For a lighter version, brush assembled samosas with oil and bake at 200C (400F) for 25-30 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden and crisp.
