Ratatouille

Cuisine: French Total: 60 min Difficulty: Medium Servings: 6 Rating: 4.7 / 5

Ratatouille is a celebration of Provencal summer at its peak. This traditional vegetable stew from Nice layers eggplant, zucchini, bell peppers, and tomatoes, each cooked to tender perfection and brought together with garlic, fresh herbs, and fruity olive oil. Whether served as a rustic stew or an elegant tian-style presentation with thinly sliced vegetables arranged in concentric circles, ratatouille is as beautiful as it is delicious.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • 1 medium eggplant, cut into 1cm rounds
  • 2 medium zucchini, cut into 1cm rounds
  • 1 yellow squash, cut into 1cm rounds
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 4 Roma tomatoes, cut into 1cm rounds
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 tbsp herbes de Provence
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the piperade base: Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large oven-safe skillet or baking dish over medium heat. Saute the diced onion for 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the diced bell peppers and cook for another 5 minutes. Stir in half the minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  2. Build the sauce: Add the crushed tomatoes, half the herbes de Provence, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly. Spread this piperade evenly across the bottom of the skillet or a large baking dish.
  3. Preheat and arrange: Preheat the oven to 190C (375F). Arrange alternating slices of eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, and Roma tomatoes in tight, slightly overlapping concentric circles (or rows) on top of the sauce, standing them nearly upright.
  4. Season the vegetables: In a small bowl, combine the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil, remaining garlic, remaining herbes de Provence, thyme leaves, salt, and pepper. Drizzle this mixture evenly over the arranged vegetables.
  5. Bake: Cover tightly with aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes until the vegetables are tender and the edges are lightly caramelized.
  6. Finish: Remove from the oven, drizzle with balsamic vinegar, and scatter fresh basil leaves over the top. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving.
  7. Serve: Serve warm as a main course with crusty bread, or as a side dish alongside grilled fish, roast chicken, or a simple omelet. Ratatouille also tastes wonderful at room temperature the next day.

Chef Tips

  • Uniform slicing: Cut all vegetables to the same thickness (about 1cm) so they cook evenly. A mandoline slicer makes quick work of this and gives a more polished presentation.
  • Salt the eggplant: For the best texture, salt eggplant slices and let them sit for 20 minutes before arranging. This draws out bitterness and excess moisture, preventing a watery dish.
  • Peak season produce: Ratatouille is a summer dish meant to showcase vegetables at their ripest. Out-of-season vegetables will yield a bland result. Make this between July and September for the best flavor.
  • Rest and reheat: Like many stews, ratatouille improves overnight as the flavors meld. Make it a day ahead and gently reheat, or serve at room temperature drizzled with fresh olive oil.
  • Olive oil quality: Use a good-quality extra-virgin olive oil here. It is a primary flavoring agent, not just a cooking medium, so its fruity, peppery character will shine through in the finished dish.
