Churros with Chocolate Sauce

Cuisine: Mexican Total: 35 min Servings: 6 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.7 / 5

Crispy on the outside, soft and doughy on the inside, and rolled in cinnamon sugar while still warm. Mexican churros are a beloved street food found at ferias and churrerias across the country, always served with a thick, rich chocolate sauce for dipping. This recipe uses a simple choux-style dough that comes together in minutes and fries up into golden, ridged perfection.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • 240ml water
  • 115g unsalted butter, cut into pieces
  • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 150g all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil, for deep frying
  • 100g granulated sugar, for coating
  • 1 tbsp ground cinnamon, for coating
  • For the chocolate sauce:
  • 150g dark chocolate (70% cacao), finely chopped
  • 120ml heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp whole milk
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of ground cayenne pepper (optional)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the dough: In a medium saucepan, combine water, butter, sugar, and salt over medium heat. Bring to a full rolling boil, ensuring the butter is completely melted. Remove from heat, add all the flour at once, and stir vigorously with a wooden spoon until the mixture forms a smooth ball that pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  2. Add eggs: Let the dough cool for 2 minutes. Beat in eggs one at a time, stirring vigorously after each until fully incorporated. Add vanilla. The dough should be thick, smooth, and pipeable. Transfer to a piping bag fitted with a large open-star tip (Wilton 1M or 8B).
  3. Heat the oil: Pour vegetable oil to a depth of 5cm in a heavy pot or Dutch oven. Heat to 190C (375F). Line a baking sheet with paper towels and set a wire rack on top.
  4. Pipe and fry: Pipe 12-15cm strips of dough directly into the hot oil, using scissors or a knife to cut each one from the tip. Fry 3-4 churros at a time for 3-4 minutes, turning once, until deep golden brown all over. Transfer to the wire rack to drain.
  5. Coat in cinnamon sugar: Mix the sugar and cinnamon in a shallow dish. While the churros are still warm, roll them in the cinnamon sugar mixture until evenly coated on all sides.
  6. Make the chocolate sauce: Heat cream and milk in a small saucepan until just simmering. Pour over chopped chocolate in a bowl and let sit 1 minute. Stir from the center outward until smooth and glossy. Add vanilla and cayenne if using. Serve warm alongside the churros.

Chef Tips

  • Star tip is essential: The ridged surface created by the star piping tip is not just decorative. The ridges increase surface area, creating more crispy edges and better cinnamon-sugar adhesion.
  • Oil temperature control: Use a thermometer and maintain 190C. If the oil drops too low, churros absorb grease and turn heavy. If too hot, the outside burns while the inside stays raw and doughy.
  • Mexican chocolate variation: For an authentic Mexican twist, use Abuelita or Ibarra Mexican chocolate tablets instead of dark chocolate. They contain cinnamon and sugar already blended in.
  • Make ahead: Pipe uncooked churros onto parchment, freeze solid, then transfer to a freezer bag. Fry directly from frozen, adding 1-2 minutes of cooking time. The dough keeps for up to 1 month.

Drink Pairing

Serve alongside a classic margarita (blanco tequila, fresh lime, triple sec) or a light Mexican lager like Modelo Especial with a salted rim. Agua fresca de jamaica (hibiscus) is the perfect non-alcoholic option.
