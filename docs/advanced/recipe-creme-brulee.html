Creme Brulee

Cuisine: French Total: 50 min + chill Difficulty: Medium Servings: 4 Rating: 4.9 / 5

Creme Brulee is the crown jewel of French desserts. Beneath its glassy, crackling caramel shell lies a silky, trembling vanilla custard that has captivated diners since it first appeared in a 1691 French cookbook. The contrast between the warm, shattered sugar crust and the cool, creamy custard below is one of the most satisfying sensations in all of pastry. With just five ingredients, this is a masterclass in simplicity and technique.

Ingredients

  • 500ml heavy cream (35% fat or higher)
  • 1 vanilla bean, split and scraped (or 2 tsp pure vanilla extract)
  • 5 large egg yolks
  • 100g granulated sugar, plus extra for the topping
  • Pinch of fine sea salt
  • Boiling water for the bain-marie

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Infuse the cream: Pour the heavy cream into a saucepan. Add the scraped vanilla seeds and the empty pod. Heat over medium heat until the cream just begins to simmer around the edges (do not boil). Remove from heat, cover, and let the vanilla steep for 15 minutes for maximum flavor extraction.
  2. Prepare the custard base: Preheat oven to 150C (300F). In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks, sugar, and salt together until pale and slightly thickened, about 2 minutes. Do not incorporate too much air.
  3. Temper and combine: Remove the vanilla pod from the cream. Slowly pour the warm cream into the egg mixture in a thin, steady stream, whisking constantly. This gradual tempering prevents the eggs from scrambling. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean jug to remove any chalazae or cooked egg bits.
  4. Fill the ramekins: Place four shallow ramekins (about 150ml each) into a deep baking dish. Divide the custard evenly among the ramekins. Skim any surface bubbles with a spoon for a perfectly smooth top.
  5. Bake in a bain-marie: Carefully pour boiling water into the baking dish until it reaches halfway up the sides of the ramekins. This water bath ensures gentle, even cooking. Bake for 35-40 minutes until the custards are set around the edges but still have a gentle wobble in the center (about 2cm diameter).
  6. Chill: Remove ramekins from the water bath and let cool to room temperature. Refrigerate uncovered for at least 4 hours or overnight. The custard must be thoroughly chilled and firmly set before torching.
  7. Brulee the top: Just before serving, sprinkle an even, thin layer (about 1 tablespoon) of granulated sugar over each custard. Using a kitchen torch, hold the flame 5-8cm above the surface and move in slow, even circles until the sugar melts, bubbles, and turns a deep amber. Let the caramel harden for 60 seconds, then serve immediately.

Chef Tips

  • Real vanilla bean: A real vanilla bean makes a noticeable difference. The tiny black seeds suspended in the custard are the visual hallmark of an authentic creme brulee. If using extract, add it after heating the cream.
  • Bain-marie is non-negotiable: The water bath insulates the custard from direct heat, preventing curdling and cracking. Without it, you will get a grainy, overcooked texture instead of silky smoothness.
  • The wobble test: When you gently shake the ramekin, the center should jiggle like set gelatin, not slosh like liquid. Residual heat will continue cooking the custard after you remove it from the oven.
  • Sugar layer thickness: The sugar for brulee-ing should be a thin, even layer. Too thick and the crust will be overly sweet and hard to crack; too thin and it will burn before caramelizing properly.
  • Serve within 30 minutes: Once you torch the sugar, the clock is ticking. The caramel will soften and lose its signature crack after about 30 minutes as it absorbs moisture from the custard.
