Classic Guacamole

Cuisine: Mexican Total: 10 min Servings: 6 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.8 / 5

True Mexican guacamole is refreshingly simple, relying on perfectly ripe avocados and a handful of fresh ingredients rather than unnecessary additions. This recipe follows the traditional method from central Mexico, where avocados are coarsely mashed in a molcajete (stone mortar) to achieve the ideal chunky texture that no food processor can replicate.

Ingredients

  • 4 ripe Hass avocados
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 1 large lime)
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 cup white onion, finely diced
  • 1 serrano pepper, seeded and minced (or jalapeño for milder heat)
  • 3 tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped
  • 1 medium Roma tomato, seeded and diced
  • 1 small clove garlic, minced to a paste (optional)
  • Pinch of ground cumin (optional)
  • Tortilla chips, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prep the onion: Soak the diced white onion in cold water for 5 minutes while you prepare the other ingredients. This removes the sharp sulfur bite while keeping the crunch. Drain and pat dry before using.
  2. Prepare the avocados: Halve each avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a wide bowl or molcajete. Add lime juice and salt immediately to prevent browning and season the base.
  3. Mash to desired texture: Using a fork or the pestle of a molcajete, mash the avocado to your preferred consistency. For the most authentic guacamole, leave it quite chunky with visible pieces of avocado throughout.
  4. Add aromatics: Fold in the drained onion, serrano pepper, and garlic (if using). Stir gently to distribute without further mashing the avocado.
  5. Finish with fresh ingredients: Add the diced tomato and chopped cilantro. Fold in gently. Taste and adjust salt, lime juice, and heat level to your preference.
  6. Serve immediately: Transfer to a serving bowl, garnish with a few cilantro leaves, and serve right away with warm tortilla chips. Press plastic wrap directly onto the surface if you need to hold it for up to 30 minutes.

Chef Tips

  • Choosing avocados: The avocado should yield to gentle pressure without feeling mushy. If the stem nub pops off easily and reveals green underneath, it is perfectly ripe. Brown underneath means overripe.
  • Prevent browning: The lime juice helps, but the best method is pressing plastic wrap directly against the surface of the guacamole, leaving no air pockets. The pit-in-the-bowl trick is a myth.
  • Salt is essential: Avocado absorbs salt, so season more generously than you think. Under-seasoned guacamole tastes flat no matter how good the avocados are.
  • Heat control: Serrano peppers are about three times hotter than jalapeños. Remove the seeds and membranes for milder heat, or leave them in for authentic Mexican spice levels.
