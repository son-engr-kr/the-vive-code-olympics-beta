Mango Sticky Rice (Khao Niao Mamuang)

Cuisine: Thai Total: 45 min Difficulty: Easy Servings: 4 Rating: 4.8 / 5

Khao Niao Mamuang is Thailand's most iconic dessert, a heavenly pairing of warm, coconut-drenched sticky rice and cool, perfectly ripe mango. Found at every Thai night market and street stall during mango season (April through June), this dessert is deceptively simple yet utterly addictive. The interplay of sweet, salty, and creamy flavors with the chewy texture of glutinous rice makes it one of the world's great desserts.

Ingredients

Showing quantities for 4 servings

  • 2 cups Thai glutinous (sticky) rice
  • 1 can (400ml) full-fat coconut milk
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 large ripe mangoes (Nam Dok Mai or Ataulfo variety preferred)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds or crispy mung beans for garnish
  • Extra coconut cream for drizzling
  • Pandan leaf (optional, for aroma)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Soak the rice: Rinse the sticky rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Cover with room-temperature water and soak for at least 4 hours or overnight. This step is essential as glutinous rice must absorb water before steaming. Do not skip it.
  2. Steam the rice: Drain the soaked rice and spread it evenly in a bamboo steamer or metal steamer basket lined with cheesecloth. Steam over rapidly boiling water for 20-25 minutes until the rice is translucent, glossy, and tender but still chewy. Flip the rice halfway through for even cooking.
  3. Prepare the coconut sauce: While the rice steams, combine the coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely and the mixture is warm (do not boil). If using a pandan leaf, add it now and remove before using. Reserve about 3 tablespoons of this mixture for the topping sauce.
  4. Soak the rice in coconut: Transfer the steamed rice to a large bowl. Pour the warm coconut sauce over the hot rice and fold gently with a spatula until every grain is coated. Cover with a lid or plate and let the rice absorb the coconut milk for 15 minutes. The rice will drink up the liquid and become lusciously creamy.
  5. Prepare the mango: Peel the mangoes and slice the cheeks off the pit. Cut each cheek into elegant slices or a crosshatch fan pattern. The mango must be perfectly ripe: fragrant, yielding to gentle pressure, and deep golden-orange throughout.
  6. Assemble: Mound a generous portion of coconut sticky rice onto each plate. Arrange the mango slices alongside. Drizzle the reserved coconut cream over the rice and mango.
  7. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds or crispy mung beans over the top for a nutty crunch. Serve immediately while the rice is still warm and the mango is cool.

Chef Tips

  • Use the right rice: You must use Thai glutinous (sticky) rice, not regular jasmine rice or sushi rice. Glutinous rice has a unique waxy starch that gives the characteristic chewy, sticky texture. It is labeled "sweet rice" or "sticky rice" at Asian grocery stores.
  • Soaking is non-negotiable: At least 4 hours of soaking is required. Under-soaked rice will be hard and chalky in the center no matter how long you steam it. Overnight soaking yields the best results.
  • Full-fat coconut milk only: Light or reduced-fat coconut milk will produce a thin, watery sauce. You need the richness of full-fat coconut milk to achieve the proper creamy, luxurious coating on the rice.
  • Mango selection: Thai Nam Dok Mai mangoes are the gold standard, but Ataulfo (honey) mangoes are an excellent substitute. The mango must be ripe and fragrant. An unripe mango will ruin the dish.
  • Temperature contrast: Serve the sticky rice warm and the mango at room temperature or slightly chilled. This temperature contrast between warm and cool is part of what makes the dessert so special.
