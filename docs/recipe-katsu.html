Chicken Katsu Curry

Cuisine: Japanese Total: 40 min Servings: 4 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.7 / 5

A beloved Japanese comfort food that pairs crispy panko-breaded chicken cutlets with a rich, mildly spiced curry sauce served over steamed short-grain rice. Katsu curry is a staple of Japanese yoshoku cuisine, blending Western influences with Japanese sensibility to create one of the most satisfying weeknight dinners imaginable.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs (about 600g total)
  • 100g all-purpose flour, seasoned with salt and pepper
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 150g panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp curry powder (S&B brand recommended)
  • 1 tbsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 500ml chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp water
  • Steamed Japanese short-grain rice, to serve
  • Pickled red ginger (beni shoga) and fukujinzuke, for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Prepare the chicken: Place each chicken thigh between sheets of cling film and pound to an even 1.5cm thickness with a meat mallet. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Bread the cutlets: Set up a three-stage breading station: flour, beaten egg, and panko. Dredge each thigh in flour, shaking off excess, dip in egg, then press firmly into panko to create a thick, even coating. Set aside on a wire rack for 5 minutes to let the coating set.
  3. Start the curry sauce: Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 minute. Stir in curry powder and garam masala, cooking for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Build the sauce: Add carrots, potatoes, and chicken stock. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes until vegetables are tender. Stir in soy sauce and honey. Add cornstarch slurry and stir until the sauce thickens to a velvety consistency.
  5. Fry the katsu: Heat 2cm of vegetable oil in a deep skillet to 170C (340F). Fry the breaded chicken for 3-4 minutes per side until deep golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature of 74C). Drain on a wire rack.
  6. Slice and serve: Let the katsu rest for 2 minutes, then slice crosswise into 1.5cm strips. Serve over steamed rice with curry sauce ladled generously alongside. Garnish with pickled ginger and fukujinzuke.

Chef Tips

  • Double-coat for extra crunch: For an exceptionally crispy katsu, dip the chicken in egg and panko twice. The double layer stays crunchy even when drenched in curry sauce.
  • Oil temperature matters: Keep your oil at a steady 170C. Too hot and the panko burns before the chicken cooks through; too cool and the coating absorbs oil and turns greasy.
  • Japanese curry blocks: For a shortcut with authentic flavor, use Japanese curry roux blocks (Vermont Curry or Golden Curry) instead of making sauce from scratch. Follow the package directions and add the same vegetables.
  • Rest the katsu: Let the fried cutlets rest on a wire rack for 2 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute and keeps the coating from getting soggy on the bottom.
