Palak Paneer

Cuisine: Indian Total: 35 min Servings: 4 Difficulty: Easy Rating: 4.7 / 5

Palak Paneer is one of North India's most beloved vegetarian dishes, featuring cubes of fresh paneer cheese nestled in a vibrant, silky spinach gravy seasoned with ginger, garlic, and warm spices. This Punjabi classic is a staple on restaurant menus worldwide, but the homemade version is brighter, fresher, and far more satisfying when you blanch the spinach properly to preserve its vivid green color.

Ingredients

  • 300g paneer, cut into 2cm cubes
  • 500g fresh baby spinach (or mature spinach, tough stems removed)
  • 2 tbsp ghee or neutral oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 green chiles (serrano or Thai), slit lengthwise
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp Kashmiri red chile powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt, plus more to taste
  • 60ml heavy cream or cashew cream
  • 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi), crushed
  • 1 tbsp butter, for finishing
  • Basmati rice or naan, for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Blanch the spinach: Bring a large pot of water to a rolling boil. Add spinach and blanch for 2 minutes until wilted and bright green. Immediately transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking and lock in the color. Squeeze out excess water and blend to a smooth puree. Set aside.
  2. Pan-fry the paneer: Heat 1 tbsp ghee in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add paneer cubes in a single layer and fry for 2 minutes per side until golden on all edges. Remove and set aside on a plate. This step adds flavor and helps the paneer hold its shape in the gravy.
  3. Build the base: In the same pan, add remaining ghee over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add onion and cook for 5-6 minutes until golden. Add garlic, ginger, and green chiles, cooking for 1 minute until aromatic.
  4. Add spices and tomato: Stir in coriander, turmeric, and Kashmiri chile powder. Cook for 30 seconds. Add pureed tomatoes and cook for 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates from the masala and the raw tomato smell disappears.
  5. Combine with spinach: Pour in the spinach puree and stir well to combine. Add salt and simmer for 5 minutes over medium-low heat. The gravy should be thick but pourable. Add a splash of water if needed to reach your preferred consistency.
  6. Finish and serve: Stir in cream, garam masala, and crushed kasuri methi. Gently fold in the fried paneer cubes. Simmer for 2 more minutes to let the paneer absorb flavor. Finish with a knob of butter on top. Serve hot with steamed basmati rice or warm naan bread.

Chef Tips

  • Ice bath is critical: Shocking the blanched spinach in ice water immediately stops the cooking and preserves the bright green color. Without this step, the dish turns an unappetizing dark olive shade.
  • Kasuri methi makes the difference: Dried fenugreek leaves add a distinctive earthy, slightly bitter aroma that is the hallmark of restaurant-quality palak paneer. Crush them between your palms before adding to release their essential oils.
  • Homemade paneer: For the freshest flavor, make paneer at home by heating 1 litre of whole milk, adding 2 tbsp lemon juice, straining the curds, and pressing for 30 minutes. Fresh paneer is softer and creamier than store-bought.
  • Vegan adaptation: Replace paneer with firm tofu (pressed and pan-fried), ghee with coconut oil, and cream with cashew cream blended from soaked raw cashews. The result is remarkably close to the original.
