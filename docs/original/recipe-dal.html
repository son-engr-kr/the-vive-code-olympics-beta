Dal Tadka (Tempered Yellow Lentils)

Cuisine: Indian Total: 30 min Difficulty: Easy Servings: 4 Rating: 4.6 / 5

Dal Tadka is one of the most beloved everyday dishes across India. Yellow lentils are simmered until creamy, then finished with a sizzling tempering (tadka) of ghee, cumin seeds, mustard seeds, dried red chilies, and garlic. The aromatic tadka poured over the dal creates an irresistible combination that pairs perfectly with steamed basmati rice or warm naan bread.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas), rinsed and soaked 15 minutes
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp salt, or to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or neutral oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 dried red chilies, broken in half
  • 1 medium onion, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish
  • Juice of half a lemon

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook the lentils: Rinse the toor dal under cold water until the water runs clear. Add the dal, 3 cups of water, turmeric, and salt to a pot. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes until the lentils are completely soft and breaking apart.
  2. Mash the dal: Using a whisk or the back of a ladle, mash the cooked lentils to a smooth, creamy consistency. Add a splash of hot water if the dal is too thick. It should be the consistency of a hearty soup.
  3. Prepare the tadka: In a small pan, heat ghee over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. When the mustard seeds begin to pop and splutter (about 30 seconds), add the dried red chilies and stir for 10 seconds.
  4. Build the aromatics: Add sliced garlic and cook until golden (about 1 minute). Add the diced onion and sauté until softened and lightly browned, about 3-4 minutes. Stir in the ginger and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add tomatoes and spices: Add the chopped tomatoes, red chili powder, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the tomatoes break down completely and the oil begins to separate from the masala, about 4-5 minutes.
  6. Combine and finish: Pour the sizzling tadka directly over the simmered dal. Stir in the garam masala and lemon juice. Let it simmer together for 2 minutes so the flavors meld.
  7. Serve: Ladle into bowls, garnish generously with fresh cilantro, and serve hot alongside steamed basmati rice or warm naan.

Chef Tips

  • Soaking the dal: While toor dal cooks relatively quickly, soaking for 15-30 minutes cuts cooking time significantly and yields a creamier texture.
  • The tadka sizzle: The tadka must be poured over the dal while it is still sizzling hot. This sizzle is what blooms the spices and creates the signature aroma of the dish.
  • Ghee matters: Ghee gives the most authentic flavor, but you can use a neutral oil for a vegan version. Avoid butter as it burns at the high heat needed for the tadka.
  • Consistency control: Dal thickens as it sits. Keep extra hot water on hand to thin it out when reheating leftovers. It tastes even better the next day.
